About me

I am currently a PhD student in the Department of Second Language Studies at the University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa. You can check out my CV for detials of my work in Applied Sociolinguistics. My PhD focus has been on linguistic landscape research, as well as some interactional sociolinguistics projects (the results of these have yet to be published). During my MA, I mostly worked on an English second language writing corpus project. In the past, I worked with Peace Corps Philippines on language related projects, as well as with the U.S. State Department on virtual English lessons in Mexico. I credit my undergraduate experience with the Myaamia Center for inspiring my professional path in language education and for my academic interests in the social influences on language. I do other things too, like rock climbing and writing bad poetry. My grandparents on both sides were part of the Hillbilly Highway.

About this website

This website was built using hexo! Hexo makes it easy to build static sites such as this in markdown. Please see the documentation for more info. The theme used is cactus. Colors are inspired by Protesilaos Stavrou’s modus theme. Previously I hosted this page using the University of Hawaiʻi’s free SFTP hosting, however I couldn’t get hexo to automatically deploy using it. I’m not sure if this was an error on my part or a limitation of UH’s hosting service, or a problem with hexo. At any rate, I am now hosting it on github pages. I plan on rewritting the website eventually and moving it to GitLab.

Hexo and github pages requires a minimal level of technical skill. If you’re just looking for an easy way to have an online portfolio, there are many options such as wix, wordpress, and google sites. I’ve used each of these in the past and they are simple to use.
